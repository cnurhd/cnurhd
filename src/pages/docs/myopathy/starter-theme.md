## 5. Antisynthetase syndrome

### 5-1. The role of Anti-synthetase

- Inhibition of tRNA synthetase in vitro
- Immune system activation
  - B cell activation
  - lFN-alpha increase
- Chemoattractant



### 5-2. Pathogenesis of ASS

(Gallay, Curr Opin Rheumatol 2018)



### 5-3. Clinical Features

- Suspect if unexplained ARDS, ILD (NSIP and/or OP), Raynaud’s phenomenon, fever, and/or Mechanic's hands

- Antisynthetase spectrum by MSA:
  - Anti-Jo-I, anti-PL 7, and anti-PL 12: the most frequent
  - Anti-Jo-i, anti—EJ, and anti-PL—7: clinically diagnosed with DM or PM
  - Anti-PL-iZ: amyopathic DM or ILD alone 
  - Anti-Ku and anti-OJ: ILD alone was common. 
  - EBA and anti-Ro/SSA may suggest myositis-related ILD

(Modified from Witt, Clin Pulm Med, 2016)

- ARDS (Severe and aggressive ILD) vs Chronic ILD



### 5-4. Treatment of ASS

- +ILD : Requires combination therapy
  - First line : prednisone + [MMF or AZA]; Consider tacrolimus as add-on therapy
  - Rituximab for patients with severe progressive and/or refractory ILD
  - CYC for ARDS
- Monitor PFT every 2~3 months for ILD progression



## 6. Immune mediated necrotizing myopathy

### 6-1. Pathogenesis in IMNM

(Allenbach, Neurology, 2018)



### 6-2. Characteristics of IMNM

- Pathology
  - Presence of necrotic fibres with scattered distribution (sometimes rare).
  - Different stages of ongoing necrosis and regeneration)
  - Macrophage predominant, pauci-lymphocytic infiltrates.
- Serology
  - Anti-SRP myopathy
  - Anti-HMGCR myopathy
  - Seronegative IMNM



### 6-3. Clinical Features

- Anti-SRP myopathy
  - Severe muscle weakness as well as often poor muscle recovery even with treatment 
  - Risk of cardiac involvement, which is rare but sometimes severe 
  - Lung involvement is frequent but usually without major clinical Significance.

- Anti-HMGCR myopathy
  - Proximal muscle weakness, markedly elevated serum CKs
  - Muscle biogsies that are usually suggestive of a necrotizing myopathy, ut occasionally can be associated with inflammatory cell infiltrates. 
  - Often associated with prior statin use, particularly in patients older than 50 years of age. 
  - Increased risk of cancer: malignancy work-up is recommended.
- CK and Anti-SRP or HMGCR : may correlate with autoantibody titers
- Malignancy Risk : seronegative >>anti-HMGCR > anti-SRP = general population
- Treatment: steroid+MTX -> RTX/ IVIG

## 9. Malignancy

- For PM/DM
  - Older age, dyspnea, male, cutaneous necrosis, cutaneous vasculitis, rapid onset of vasculitis, Higher CK/ESR/CRP
- For dm
  - Older age, Male, cutaneous necrosis, Higher ESR/CRP, TIF1y
- Decreasing risk
  - ILD, Arthralgia/arthritis, RP, Anti-Jo1

(Lu, Plos One, 2014)
